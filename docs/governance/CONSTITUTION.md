# Preamble

This Constitution establishes the governing principles, standards, and
philosophy of the College Football ATS Research Platform. It exists to
ensure that every dataset, workflow, research study, and software
component is developed with integrity, transparency, reproducibility,
and long-term maintainability.

This document is the supreme governing document of the project. All
other policies, standards, manuals, and implementation details derive
their authority from this Constitution.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Article I --- Mission

The mission of the project is to build a comprehensive, reproducible
research platform for NCAA FBS against-the-spread (ATS) analysis using
high-quality data, documented methodology, and transparent statistical
practices.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Article II --- Core Principles

1.  **Truth Before Conclusions** --- Research follows the evidence.
2.  **Reproducibility** --- Every published result must be reproducible.
3.  **Transparency** --- Methods, assumptions, and formulas are
    documented.
4.  **Data Integrity** --- Raw source data is never modified.
5.  **Continuous Improvement** --- The platform evolves through
    version-controlled revisions.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Article III --- Governance

The Constitution is the highest governing document.

The following documents derive from it:

-   Project Manifest
-   ATS Policy
-   Data Dictionary
-   Research Dictionary
-   Data Acquisition Manual
-   API Endpoint Reference
-   Reference Tables

Conflicts are resolved in favor of the higher-order governing document.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Article IV --- Data Governance

The platform shall maintain distinct stages of data:

1.  Raw Data
2.  Processed Data
3.  Master Database
4.  Research Outputs

Raw data is preserved indefinitely to ensure complete reproducibility.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Article V --- Research Standards

Research shall:

-   Use documented methodologies.
-   Record assumptions.
-   Distinguish observation from interpretation.
-   Report sample sizes.
-   Avoid overstating statistical significance.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Article VI --- Software Standards

Automation, scripts, and workflows shall:

-   Be version controlled.
-   Be reproducible.
-   Be documented.
-   Prefer proof-of-concept validation before expansion.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Bill of Definitions

The official meanings of project terminology are maintained by the
Research Dictionary and Data Dictionary. Those documents provide the
canonical vocabulary used throughout the platform.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Research Oath

Every analysis performed within this platform shall prioritize accuracy,
transparency, and reproducibility over convenience or desired outcomes.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Founder's Note

The project is intended to become a long-term research platform whose
value comes from disciplined methodology, well-documented decisions, and
continuous refinement rather than any individual result.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
